# Solution Design

## MagnaStep — The Sports Inclusion Platform

**Type:** For-profit startup (B-corp certified)
**Headquartered:** Kigali, Rwanda
**Mission:** Make high-performance adaptive sport accessible to every amputee in sub-Saharan Africa, regardless of income.

---

## The Insight

Standard prosthetics are designed for mobility — walking, working, daily life. For most amputees, they are sufficient. MagnaStep is not for everyone. It is specifically for young amputees who want to move athletically — to run, board, cycle, climb, compete. The technology only makes sense if the sporting aspiration exists. That aspiration is our market.

---

## The Three-Part Solution

### 1. The Device — MagnaStep Sport Prosthetic
A modular magnetic sport prosthetic engineered for dynamic physical activity:
- Magnetic osseointegration coupling: attaches directly and securely, allowing natural range of motion
- Modular design: one base implant, swappable sport-specific end attachments (running blade, board binding, cycling clip)
- Manufactured using regional supply chains to reduce cost — target price $2,000–$4,000 vs. $15,000–$70,000 for equivalent Western devices
- Designed for the climate, terrain, and body profiles of sub-Saharan African users

### 2. The Fitting & Support Network — Regional Partnership Model
MagnaStep does not build clinics from scratch. Instead:
- **Disability services partnerships:** Embed within existing disability organisations and rehabilitation centres across Rwanda, Uganda, and Kenya. Train local technicians in fitting, calibration, and ongoing adjustment. MagnaStep provides the device and training; partners provide the infrastructure and patient relationships.
- **Annual check-in programme:** Remote monitoring via a simple mobile app (basic smartphone compatible) allowing technicians to flag fit issues and order adjustments without the patient travelling far.

### 3. The Sports Pathway — Inclusion Partnerships
- **Sports organisation partnerships:** Work with national football federations, athletics boards, and schools to create inclusive training programmes where adaptive athletes train alongside able-bodied peers — not in separate leagues, but in the same clubs.
- **Adaptive sports coaching:** MagnaStep-certified coaches embedded in partner sports clubs, trained to work with prosthetic users at all levels.
- **Pathway to competition:** Clear progression from local club → national adaptive team → Paralympic qualification. MagnaStep athletes become brand ambassadors and proof points.

---

## Business Model

| Revenue Stream | Description |
|---|---|
| Device sales | Subsidised pricing model — patients pay on a sliding scale based on income; gap funded through partnerships |
| NGO & impact investor grants | Fund device R&D and initial market entry |
| Sports organisation partnerships | Clubs and federations pay a programme fee for inclusive coaching integration |
| Government & disability service contracts | Supply agreements with national disability services in Rwanda, Uganda, Kenya |

**The key distinction:** MagnaStep is a startup, not a charity. It is designed to be commercially viable while serving a segment that traditional prosthetics companies ignore. B-corp certification ensures the social mission is legally protected as the company scales.

---

## Why This Works

- Disability services provide trust, reach, and existing patient relationships — no cold customer acquisition
- Sports organisations have inclusion mandates and budgets — they are motivated partners, not charity cases
- The modular device design keeps costs low and allows athletes to expand their kit as they progress
- Amara's story — and thousands like hers — is the most powerful marketing asset the company has
