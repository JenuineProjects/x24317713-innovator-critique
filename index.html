Our Inspiration

Meet Amara.

"I always assumed that world wasn't for me. MagnaStep made me realise the only thing holding me back was access."

Amara grew up tending her family's tomato farm in the hills of Rwanda. She lost her leg as a child. Every evening she'd watch snowboarders on her phone — that mix of speed and freedom — and quietly let the dream go.

Then she heard about magnetic osseointegration: prosthetics responsive enough for dynamic sport. The technology existed. But it cost $70,000 and was only available in Europe. For Amara, it might as well have been on another planet.

MagnaStep was built for Amara. And for the thousands like her across Rwanda, Uganda, and Kenya whose aspiration has always been bigger than their access.

The Problem

The barrier was never the body.

Young amputees across sub-Saharan Africa are physically capable of competing at the highest levels. Three things stand in their way.

💰 Cost

High-performance magnetic prosthetics cost $15,000–$70,000 in Western markets — completely out of reach for most families in the region.

📍 Geography

Specialist fitting clinics exist only in Europe and North America. Travelling there for fittings and adjustments is logistically and financially impossible for most patients.

🏅 Pathway

Even where prosthetics are available, there is no structured route from rehabilitation into sport — no clubs, no coaches, no clear progression toward competition.

The Solution

Three parts. One mission.

MagnaStep is not just a prosthetic. It is a complete system built around the athlete — not the device.

🧲

The Device

A modular magnetic sport prosthetic engineered for dynamic activity — running, boarding, cycling. Manufactured regionally. Target price: $2,000–$4,000. One base implant, swappable sport-specific attachments.

🏥

The Fitting Network

Embedded within existing disability services and rehabilitation centres across Rwanda, Uganda, and Kenya. Local technicians trained by MagnaStep. No need to travel to Europe. Remote support via mobile.

🏆

The Sports Pathway

Partnerships with national sports organisations to create genuinely inclusive training — adaptive athletes alongside able-bodied peers. Local club to national team to Paralympic qualification.
